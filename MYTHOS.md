# Mythos — Ribbon

There's a strip of touchsensitive material that runs the length of the Korg Monotribe. You press it and sound comes out — pitch determined by where, expressiveness determined by how. No keys. No frets. Just a ribbon and the pressure of your finger.

That object is the whole idea.

Ribbon began as a question: what if that instrument lived in the browser? Not emulated — *translated*. A gesture interface for synthesis that any device with a screen could become. A thing you play, not a thing you configure.

It grew. Oscillators, filters, effects, presets. The architecture expanded while the core stayed the same: press here, hear that. The gap between hand and sound is the design surface.

Ribbon is the trunk. Other projects have forked from it — puddle toward ambience, grains toward texture — but ribbon stays playable. It's the place where the idea started and the place it keeps returning to.

Whatever else it becomes, it should feel like picking something up.
